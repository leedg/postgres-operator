# Maintainers

This document tracks the maintainers of keiailab/postgres-operator, who
hold decision and merge authority for the project.

## Current maintainers

| Name / team | GitHub | Role | Area |
|---|---|---|---|
| keiailab maintainers | [@keiailab/maintainers](https://github.com/orgs/keiailab/teams/maintainers) | Lead | Whole project |

The GitHub team `@keiailab/maintainers` holds merge and approval rights
across every area. Individual maintainers are added through the process
below.

## Maintainer eligibility

A contributor who has met the following bar for at least six months may be
nominated for maintainer:

- ≥ 20 merged PRs (meaningful code or documentation contributions).
- ≥ 30 reviewed PRs (with constructive feedback).
- Adherence to this project's [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md) and
  [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
- Deep understanding of at least one core area (controller, instance
  manager, backup, Citus integration, docs, etc.).

## Adding a maintainer

1. An existing maintainer or the candidate themself opens a proposal
   (issue or RFC).
2. The `@keiailab/maintainers` team applies lazy consensus over a 7-day
   comment window.
3. With no objection, add the new maintainer to the GitHub team and update
   MAINTAINERS.md via a PR.

## Inactive maintainers

A maintainer who has been inactive for six consecutive months moves to
emeritus status (privileges revoked, name preserved in the emeritus
roster). Returning follows the same procedure as the initial addition.

## Area ownership (mirrors CODEOWNERS)

See `/.github/CODEOWNERS`. Per-directory automatic reviewers are assigned
from that file.
